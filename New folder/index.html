Turtle Hatcheries possess a significant importance in the conservation of Sea Turtle Populations in Sri Lanka. Due to various human activities such as habitat destruction, illegal trading/ poaching and other predators, turtles are currently facing a high risk of extinction. Species such as the Leatherback, Hawksbill, Olive Ridley, Loggerhead and the Green Turtle are found in Sri Lanka.



The purpose of Turtle Hatcheries is to provide a safe environment for turtle egg hatching, whilst also increasing their chances of survival in order to protect and retain the turtle population of Sri Lanka. They are also used as a method of educating the general public about the importance of the conservation of Sea Turtles by offering various forms of Tour Guides, Presentation Videos and more
